WHAT DOES A TYPICAL SEEDING PROCESS LOOK LIKE?

In the previous article, we learned what seeding is and the important role it plays in a successful marketing campaign. So, what steps are needed to carry out a seeding campaign? Your questions will be answered in the following article:

Receiving Information from the Client

After receiving the client's request, you need to analyze the goals, messages, and target audience the brand wants to approach, from which you can find a suitable strategy for your seeding campaign.

Planning

To have a comprehensive and overall plan, you need to brainstorm with your team to come up with the best plan to send to the client.
What needs to be clearly defined from the start are the 5W+H (What, Where, When, Why, Who, How), the expected results and goals, and the implementation steps for executing the campaign.

Creating Topics and Content to Seed Discussions

The content created needs to be attractive and engaging but should also be natural and authentic to generate lively discussions and strong interest from the community. These topics and content should closely align with the message and direction the brand wants to convey to consumers.

Submitting Content for Client Approval

After having a complete plan and content topics, the next step is to send it to the client for editing and feedback regarding the content, methods, and main communication channels to be used. This helps both parties have a common voice, agree on goals and direction to achieve maximum work efficiency.

Working with Media Channels

Once the entire idea and plan are agreed upon with the client, the next step is to implement the work. You need to contact media channels to post according to the schedule and seed comments following the prepared script.

Predicting Risks and Handling Crises if Any

Communication crises are always a risk that can occur at any time during a marketing campaign. To limit and handle this issue, you should prepare backup plans to deal with it. This will help you always be proactive, control the situation, and resolve crises easily and timely, rescuing your client from unexpected situations.

Monitoring, Evaluating, and Reporting

After executing according to plan and handling arising issues, it is time for you to monitor and compile, evaluate interaction metrics such as likes, shares, and the community’s interest in the brand, comparing them with the KPIs set by the brand before starting the project. Finally, send the acceptance report summarizing the results achieved from project implementation and close the campaign.
The above are the 7 basic steps needed in a seeding marketing campaign. Of course, depending on the situation and actual conditions of each campaign, we will flexibly resolve and execute according to the specific needs of each client. If you understand and perform this seeding tool well, it will be a supreme weapon that helps your marketing campaign become brilliantly successful.
